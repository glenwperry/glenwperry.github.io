About Me

Some Answers To Your Questions

About Me:

I’m a corporate governance expert with 5+ years experience. I’ve managed diverse teams at home and abroad and supervised the publication of high-level research for institutional investor clients. I’m regarded by my colleagues as a patient and easy-to-work-with collaborator who is particularly skilled at analyzing complicated shareholder conflicts.

What I’m Looking For:

The opportunity to leverage my corporate governance and proxy voting expertise in a place with upward growth opportunities.

How I’ve Invested In Myself:

I created this website to showcase some of my technical abilities and am always looking to further my HTML/CSS/Bootstrap/jQuery skills. I've taken courses with EdX such as Excel for Data Analysis and Visualization and HTML5. I seek out opportunities to broaden my horizons and meet new people, as evidenced by my wide range of volunteer activities - from providing free tutoring services to planting big cone pine trees to helping build protected bike lanes throughout L.A..

What Drives Me:

My passion is to understand people, including myself. I think it's important to keep a healthy desire to learn about anything and everything. I’m interested in all the secret human gunk that makes a life. I’m passionate about the aesthetic experiences, cultural tides and financial mechanisms that form our world on a personal and global scale.

If We Had a Band:

My role in the band would be pipe organist and we would play baroque horror punk. If I were to injure my fingers I would tell the rest of the band that they need to temporarily find another pipe organist but that I’m already teaching myself the theremin and that I’d still love to come to the gig up in New Brunswick.

Why These Questions:

I'm trying to give my future employer a feel for my personality across a broader spectrum than "where do you see yourself in ten years?" provides. I want to find a position in which I am a good fit, and which is a good fit for me, and I feel this is a better way of achieving it than providing a template cover letter using lots of Linkedin buzzwords.
