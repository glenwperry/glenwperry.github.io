A Skill I’ve Learned:

I’ve been writing creatively as a hobby all my life and it’s something I’m always trying to get better at. My main interest is in literary fiction (Lorrie Moore, Updike, Franzen) and I’ve put a lot of time into honing my skills in this area. I’m currently 30,000 words into a full-length fictional novel that I’ve been working on for about one year. I’ve also attended night classes at UCLA extension to get myself involved in a community of likeminded writers. The biggest challenge is stomping down all the nagging doubt and worry that I’m wasting my time with this hobby when I could be learning something that might be more readily monetized. When working on a technically or emotionally challenging passage, rather than quitting, I have to look to the future and the end satisfaction of writing something that I feel is "true" - even if it's made up.

What Drives Me:

My passion is to understand people, including myself. I do this through writing, through reading literary fiction, through physical endurace (backpacking, cycling), and by keeping a healthy desire to learn about anything and everything. I basically want to be Benjamin Franklin. I’m endlessly interested in all the secret human gunk that makes a life. I’m passionate about the aesthetic experiences (film scores, smells, love letters etc.) that hit people right in the stomach and remind them that they’re far from alone on this planet.
